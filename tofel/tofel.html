Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL)

The TOEFL is a standardized test that measures the English language proficiency of non-native speakers, required for admission to many universities worldwide. It is conducted by ETS.

When Should You Take the TOEFL?

Students planning to study abroad should take the TOEFL at least 3-6 months before their application deadlines.

Detailed Syllabus

  • Reading: Comprehension of academic passages.
  • Listening: Understanding lectures and conversations.
  • Speaking: Expressing opinions and summarizing information.
  • Writing: Essay writing and summarizing tasks.

Preparation Tips

  • Practice with official ETS materials and sample tests.
  • Improve listening and speaking skills by engaging in conversations and listening to English media.
  • Work on time management to complete each section efficiently.
  • Enhance vocabulary and grammar through regular reading and writing.

Features of TOEFL Exam

  • Available in internet-based (iBT) and paper-based formats.
  • Score validity: 2 years.
  • Accepted by over 11,000 institutions worldwide.
  • Can be taken multiple times with a 12-day gap between attempts.
